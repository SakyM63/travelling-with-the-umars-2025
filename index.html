Travelling with the Umars

A Umar-velous 2025 Journey

Part 1: Spain

Seville: A Grand Andalusian Welcome

Day 1

Friday, September 26 | Departure from Vancouver

  • Departure from Vancouver (YVR) on Air Canada flight AC 302 at 7:40 AM, arriving in Montreal (YUL) at 3:35 PM.
  • Departure from Montreal (YUL) on Air Canada flight AC 9634 at 6:05 PM, arriving in Frankfurt (FRA) at 7:10 AM the next day.

Day 2

Saturday, September 27 | Arrival in Seville

  • Departure from Frankfurt (FRA) on Air Canada flight AC 9158 at 8:35 AM, arriving in Seville (SVQ) at 11:30 AM.
  • Transfer from the airport to accommodation.
  • In the afternoon, settling in and exploring begins with a stroll through the Plaza de España.
  • The evening includes enjoying a sunset at the Metropol Parasol and having dinner with tapas in the historic Barrio Santa Cruz.

Day 3

Sunday, September 28 | Seville

  • Checking into Hotel Fernando III, for a stay from Sunday, September 28, to Tuesday, September 30.
  • The morning is for visiting the Royal Alcázar of Seville.
  • In the afternoon, exploring the Seville Cathedral and climbing the Giralda Tower for panoramic city views.
  • The evening features a traditional flamenco show.

Day 4

Monday, September 29 | Seville

  • A full day to explore more of Seville.
  • Considerations include visiting the Museo de Bellas Artes or the vibrant Triana neighborhood across the river.
  • Taking a tapas tour or a cooking class is also an option.
  • The evening concludes with a final dinner in Seville.

Day 5

Tuesday, September 30 | Seville to Granada

  • In the morning, taking the Renfe AVE train departing Seville-Santa Justa at 10:30 AM to Granada.
  • The journey is about 2.5-3 hours.
  • In the afternoon, checking into the Alhambra Palace Hotel in Granada for a stay from Tuesday, September 30, to Thursday, October 2.
  • Exploring the picturesque Albaicín, the old Arab quarter, once settled.
  • The evening is for dinner at a restaurant in the Albaicín.

Granada: Moorish Majesty

Day 6

Wednesday, October 1 | Granada

  • A full day to visit the Alhambra, the most famous landmark in Granada.
  • Tickets should be booked well in advance, as they sell out quickly.
  • A full day is needed to explore the Nasrid Palaces, Generalife Gardens, and the Alcazaba fortress.
  • The evening can be spent relaxing and enjoying local tapas in the city center.

Day 7

Thursday, October 2 | Granada

  • Checking out of the Alhambra Palace Hotel and checking into the Parador de Granada for a one-night stay, including breakfast.
  • In the morning, visiting the Granada Cathedral and the adjacent Royal Chapel, the final resting place of Queen Isabella I and King Ferdinand II.
  • The afternoon is for exploring the Corral del Carbón and the bustling Alcaicería.
  • In the evening, considering a visit to a "teteria" (tea house) for a traditional Moroccan tea experience.

Day 8

Friday, October 3 | Granada to Cordoba

  • Checking out of the Parador de Granada.
  • Checking into the Parador de Córdoba for a stay from October 3 to October 6, including breakfast.
  • Travel by Renfe AVE train departing Granada at 3:06 PM to Cordoba.
  • The journey is typically about 1.5-2 hours.
  • After checking in, exploration begins with a visit to the Mezquita-Cathedral.
  • The evening is for wandering through the charming Jewish Quarter (Judería).

Cordoba: A Fusion of Cultures

Day 9

Saturday, October 4 | Cordoba

  • In the morning, visiting the Alcázar of the Christian Monarchs.
  • The afternoon is for a guided tour of the famous Patios de Córdoba.
  • In the evening, enjoying a relaxing dinner and another flamenco show.

Day 10

Sunday, October 5 | Cordoba

  • A trip to the ruins of Medina Azahara, a 10th-century medieval Muslim city, is planned for the morning.
  • The afternoon is for indulging in relaxation at a traditional Hammam, such as Hammam Al Andalus.
  • The final night in Spain will be enjoyed with a farewell dinner.

Day 11

Monday, October 6 | Travel from Spain to Marrakesh

  • Checking out of the Parador de Córdoba.
  • Enjoying a final breakfast in Cordoba.
  • In the afternoon, taking the Renfe train departing Cordoba at 5:49 PM to Seville-Santa Justa.
  • In the evening, transferring to San Pablo Airport (SVQ) for a flight to Marrakesh.
  • Departure from Seville on Ryanair flight FR4008 at 9:55 PM, arriving in Marrakesh (RAK) at 10:20 PM.
  • Transfer to the hotel.

Part 2: Morocco

Marrakesh: The Red City

Day 12

Tuesday, October 7 | Marrakesh

  • Checking into Savoy Le Grand Hotel Marrakech.
  • In the morning, exploring the famous Majorelle Garden.
  • The afternoon is for visiting the Yves Saint Laurent Museum, located nearby.
  • The evening includes a sunset camel ride in the Agafay Desert with a traditional Berber dinner and show.

Day 13

Wednesday, October 8 | Marrakesh

  • In the morning, getting lost in the maze-like streets of the Marrakesh Medina and visiting the Bahia Palace.
  • The afternoon is for haggling for souvenirs and spices in the bustling souks of the Medina.
  • In the evening, heading back to Jemaa el-Fnaa square to see food stalls, snake charmers, and street performers.

Day 14

Thursday, October 9 | Marrakesh

  • Another day to be enjoyed in Marrakesh.
  • Options include a day trip to the Ouzoud Waterfalls or the Ourika Valley, or spending more time exploring the city's hidden gems.
  • The evening concludes with a final meal in Marrakesh.

Day 15

Friday, October 10 | Marrakesh to Fez

  • Checking out of the Savoy Le Grand Hotel Marrakech.
  • Departure from Marrakesh (RAK) on Ryanair flight FR6731 at 11:45 AM, arriving in Fez (FEZ) at 12:45 PM.
  • After arriving in Fez, transferring to accommodation.
  • Checking into a Deluxe Suite at Riad Maison Bleue & Spa.
  • In the evening, exploring the Fez el-Bali and finding a traditional Moroccan restaurant for dinner.

Fez: The Imperial City

Day 16

Saturday, October 11 | Fez

  • In the morning, taking a guided tour of the Fez el-Bali, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the largest car-free urban areas in the world.
  • The afternoon is for visiting the iconic Chouara Tannery.
  • The evening includes a stroll through the Henna Souk and other artisan quarters.

Day 17

Sunday, October 12 | Fez

  • The morning is for visiting the Al Attarine Madrasa.
  • The afternoon can be spent relaxing, shopping for last-minute souvenirs, or visiting the impressive Bab Bou Jeloud (the Blue Gate).
  • In the evening, a final night in Fez will be savored with a delicious Moroccan feast.

Day 18

Monday, October 13 | Fez to Casablanca

  • Checking out of Riad Maison Bleue.
  • Travel by high-speed train from Fez to Casablanca, a journey of approximately 3.5-4 hours.
  • Checking into the Hyatt Regency Casablanca.
  • In the evening, a stroll along the La Corniche waterfront promenade and finding a place to eat with a view of the Atlantic Ocean.

Casablanca: The Modern Metropolis

Day 19

Tuesday, October 14 | Casablanca

  • In the morning, visiting the majestic Hassan II Mosque.
  • The afternoon is for exploring the Old Medina and the Habous Quarter, also known as the New Medina.
  • In the evening, enjoying a farewell dinner in Casablanca before departure.

Day 20

Wednesday, October 15 | Departure from Casablanca

  • Checking out of the Hyatt Regency Casablanca.
  • Depending on flight time, a visit to the Cathédrale Sacré Cœur may be possible.
  • Transfer to Mohammed V International Airport (CMN) for a flight home to Vancouver (YVR).
  • Departure from Casablanca (CMN) on Air Canada flight AC 9430 at 1:25 AM, arriving in Frankfurt (FRA) at 5:50 AM.
  • Departure from Frankfurt (FRA) on Air Canada flight AC 9101 at 1:20 PM, arriving in Vancouver (YVR) at 2:20 PM.

Day 21

Thursday, October 16 | Arrival in Vancouver

  • Arrival back in Vancouver, concluding the journey through Spain and Morocco.
